Before You Play

The Courses

Three completely different rounds on the same 1,200 acres. Here's what you're walking into.

Cabot Citrus Farms sits on the old World Woods property in Brooksville — an hour north of Tampa, on a prehistoric sand ridge the Gulf of Mexico once ran through. Cabot bought it in 2021 and rebuilt almost everything from the ground up. You'll play three of its four courses this week. The practice facility has a TrackMan range and opens at 7:30 AM — good for a warm-up before the early tees.

Karoo

“Wide-open, sand-blown, unapologetically bold.”
History

Built over Tom Fazio's old Pine Barrens course, once a national top-100 fixture before it faded. When Cabot took over, they handed architect Kyle Franz — a Doak/Hanse protégé fresh off acclaimed Sandhills restorations — his first solo design, and let him run.

Style

Franz calls it “adventure golf” — thousands of trees cleared, sand exposed everywhere, fairways up to 100+ yards wide, with 11 double fairways and a triple at the 18th. Named for the sandhill crane's rattling call you'll hear all day.

Difficulty

7,562 yards, par 72, slope 149 from the tips. The width forgives a bad drive; the severely contoured greens do not — expect three-putts from makeable range.

Inside Info

Pick your side of the fairway on purpose — the splits set up completely different angles into the green. Play safe off the tee, aggressive with the putter.

Roost

“The quieter, prettier sibling.”
History

Laid over the old Rolling Oaks routing by a rare four-architect collaboration — Kyle Franz, historian Ran Morrissett, Cabot Links designer Rod Whitman, and Mike Nuzzo — built a year behind Karoo so the team could make it deliberately different.

Style

Named for the wild turkeys that roost in the property's moss-draped live oaks. Undulating ground, creeks, a big lake, and restrained bunkering — several holes carry no sand at all.

Difficulty

7,032 yards, par 72. Less sand to think about than Karoo, but the old oaks demand more precise shot-shaping off the tee.

Inside Info

This is the round to actually slow down on — enjoy the lake holes on the back nine instead of grinding over every shot.

The Wedge

“Only club you need is the one it's named for.”
History

Designed by Mike Nuzzo on a spare 120-acre parcel as the baby sibling to his 10-hole Squeeze course next door.

Style

11 holes, par 33, nothing longer than about 110 yards — but packed with the same huge waste areas and wildly sloped greens as the big courses, just shrunk down. Lit for night golf year-round, tee times as late as 9pm, with a soft glow instead of harsh floodlights.

Difficulty

Short doesn't mean easy — the greens are the whole game here, and there's sand on nearly every hole.

Inside Info

This is exactly where you're playing Thursday afternoon — as the sun drops, the waste bunkers throw long shadows that mess with your depth perception. Leave the driver in the cart.

The Squeeze

“Short on time, big on drama.”
History

Mike Nuzzo's 10-hole short course — the bigger sibling to The Wedge next door. Where The Wedge is all wedges, The Squeeze stretches out with real par 4s and even a par 5, built to deliver a full-golf feeling in a fraction of the time.

Style

Nuzzo drew on the world's best short par 4s and half-par holes — ten holes across constantly varied terrain, each asking for a different plan off the tee. Cabot's own words: “full of fun and drama.”

Difficulty

Par 38 over holes running anywhere from 100 to 550 yards. The half-par holes reward a bit of nerve — take on the green and you're rewarded, bail out and you'll grind for par.

Inside Info

The one course not on this week's card — but the easy add-on. It keeps golf carts year-round, so it's the low-effort way to squeeze in a few extra holes.

Getting There

Travel

Fly into Tampa, point the car north, and you're on property in under an hour.

Cabot Citrus Farms sits outside Brooksville on Florida's Nature Coast — about an hour north of Tampa. Fly in, grab a car, and cruise up the Suncoast Parkway. Land by early Thursday afternoon so nobody misses the 3:36 PM first tee.

Fly Into

Tampa · TPA

Tampa International is the closest major airport — roughly a 55-minute drive north. Orlando (MCO) works as a backup at about 1½ hours.

The Drive

≈ 50 miles

Straight up the Suncoast Parkway (FL-589) into Brooksville. Grab a rental or carpool — sort out rides so no one's stranded at baggage claim.

Concierge · Maybe

Cabot Citrus Concierge

We may skip the rentals entirely and book the resort's Cabot Citrus Concierge to shuttle the whole group from Tampa (TPA) to Cabot and back — door to door, no designated driver. The concierge desk is back August 3rd, so we can't lock it in until then.
